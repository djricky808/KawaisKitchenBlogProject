Beef Tripe Curry Stew

An unexpected delicacy

What is Beef Tripe?

Tripe is a type of meat that is the lining of the cow's stomach. The tripe used in this recipe is known as honeycomb tripe, which is located in the reticulum chamber of the cow's stomach. Tripe can also be found in other ruminant animals, such as sheep, goat, lamb, etc.

Tripe is not just limited for use in stews. It can be used in sausages too, such and andouille.

How to clean beef tripe

When you buy fresh tripe, there's a natural scent to it, that some people, like me can find unpleasant. There is a way to wash the tripe however, to get rid of some of the smell, and provide some flavor when you are making the beef stew.

To wash it, submerge the tripe in water mixed with Hawaiian Salt. Massage the tripe while submerged in water (also referred to as lomi lomi which means massage).

After you are done rinsing, we can cut the tripe up into bite size pieces

Beef Curry Tripe Stew

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of Beef honeycomb tripe
  • 1 Tablespoon Hawaiian Salt
  • 2 potatoes (peeled and chopped)
  • 3 medium size carrots (peeled and chopped)
  • 2-3 stalks of celery (chopped)
  • 1 small round onion (chopped)
  • 2 blocks of S&B curry mix (I like to use mild)
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 cup of beef broth (or stock)
  • 2 tablespoons of butter (melted)
  • 1 teaspoon Garlic Salt

Instructions

  1. Rinse out the tripe by submerging it in water with the Hawaiian Salt, lomi lomi (massage) the tripe briefly before removing it from the water.
  2. Cut the tripe into bite size pieces, then add them to your pressure cooker pot
  3. Add chopped vegetables to the pot
  4. Melt your S&B curry blocks in a pyrex measuring cup with 1/4 cup of water (or enough to submerge the curry blocks) in the microwave for 1 minute
  5. Add the curry and all remaining ingredient to the pot and stir well
  6. Set pressure cooker on High for 45 minutes (you can also do 30 minutes, but the longer you cook it, the softer the tripe will be)
  7. Let the pressure cooker naturally release (leave it alone) for 10 minute before releasing the remaining pressure
  8. Serve with rice and enjoy

If your curry is little too runny, a slurry can be added by mixing equal parts of some of the curry with cornstarch. You can also add some more salt (if needed)
